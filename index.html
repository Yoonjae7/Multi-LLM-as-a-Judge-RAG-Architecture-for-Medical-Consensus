Multi-LLM-as-a-Judge RAG Architecture for Medical Consensus

A multi-agent healthcare AI framework combining Retrieval-Augmented Generation, Agentic AI, and LLM-as-a-Judge to deliver evidence-grounded, explainable health information across Western Medicine, Traditional Chinese Medicine, Nutrition, and Lifestyle Medicine.

Research Problem

Current healthcare AI assistants suffer from critical limitations.

  • Hallucinations — Models generate plausible but unsupported medical claims without grounding in authoritative sources.
  • Lack of evidence transparency — Responses rarely cite clinical guidelines, literature, or explain the strength of underlying evidence.
  • Conflicting paradigms — Systems cannot reconcile recommendations from different medical traditions and knowledge bases.
  • Poor safety governance — Unsafe advice, contraindications, and unsupported claims often pass through without automated review.
  • Limited explainability — Users receive single opaque answers rather than transparent reasoning, uncertainty, or disagreement.

Research Vision

To develop a trustworthy and explainable healthcare AI ecosystem that intelligently integrates knowledge from multiple medical paradigms, verifies recommendations through evidence-aware AI judges, and provides transparent, safety-conscious health guidance — transforming healthcare AI from a single-answer chatbot into a virtual multidisciplinary advisory board.

Research Questions

Key questions guiding the investigation.

  1. Can multi-agent retrieval and reasoning improve the factual accuracy and completeness of healthcare question answering compared with conventional RAG systems?
  2. Can LLM-as-a-Judge mechanisms effectively detect hallucinations, unsafe recommendations, and unsupported claims in healthcare AI outputs?
  3. How can AI systems reconcile recommendations from different medical paradigms while maintaining transparency and user trust?
  4. Does multi-agent debate and consensus-building improve healthcare answer quality compared with single-agent generation?
  5. How should healthcare AI communicate evidence strength, uncertainty, and conflicting viewpoints to end users?
  6. Can integrative healthcare AI increase user confidence and perceived trustworthiness without sacrificing scientific rigor?
